Thorough Termite Inspection

A meticulous inspection is the foundation of effective termite control. Our certified technicians are trained to identify even subtle signs of termite activity, including:

Signs of Termite Presence

  • Mud tubes: These are small tunnels made of mud and wood fibers that termites use to travel between their colony and their food source.
  • Swarming termites: The presence of winged termites, especially in or around your home or business, is a strong indicator of a nearby colony.
  • Discarded wings: After swarming, termites shed their wings, which can often be found near windowsills or light fixtures.
  • Hollow-sounding wood: Tapping on wood that sounds hollow suggests that termites have been eating away at the interior.
  • Frass: This is the fecal matter of drywood termites, which resembles small pellets and can be found near infested wood.

Our inspection goes beyond the obvious. We will carefully examine the perimeter of your property, crawl spaces, attics, basements, and any areas where wood comes into contact with the soil. This detailed assessment allows us to pinpoint the location and severity of the infestation and develop a precise treatment strategy.

Customized Treatment Plans

Based on the findings of our inspection, we will develop a customized treatment plan that is specifically designed to eliminate the termites infesting your property. Our plans take into account the type of termite, the extent of the infestation, the construction of your building, and your individual needs and preferences. We will explain the proposed treatment methods in detail and answer any questions you may have, ensuring you are fully informed and comfortable with the process.

Effective Termite Treatment Methods

We utilize a variety of proven termite treatment methods, including:

Subterranean Termite Treatment

Subterranean termites are the most common and often the most destructive type of termite. Our treatment methods for subterranean termites typically involve creating a protective barrier around your property. This may include liquid termiticides applied to the soil around the foundation, or the installation of termite baiting systems. These methods are highly effective in eliminating existing colonies and preventing future infestations.

Drywood Termite Treatment

Drywood termites infest dry wood above ground and do not require contact with the soil. Treatment methods for drywood termites may include localized treatments to infested areas, such as wood injection treatments or microwave technology. In some cases, fumigation may be necessary for widespread drywood termite infestations throughout an entire structure. We will recommend the most appropriate treatment method based on the specific situation.

Tent Fumigation

For severe or widespread drywood termite infestations, tent fumigation may be the most effective solution. This process involves covering the structure with a large tent and introducing a fumigant gas that penetrates deep into the wood to kill all termites present. While requiring temporary evacuation, fumigation provides a comprehensive solution for eradicating drywood termites from the entire building.

Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

Our approach incorporates Integrated Pest Management principles, focusing on long-term prevention and sustainable solutions. This includes identifying and addressing factors that attract termites, such as moisture problems or wood-to-soil contact. By addressing these underlying issues, we help reduce the risk of future infestations.

Our commitment to protecting your property doesn't end with the initial treatment. We offer post-treatment monitoring and prevention services to ensure that termites do not return. This may include follow-up inspections and recommendations for preventative measures you can take to minimize the risk of re-infestation.
